# Praxis tagline matrix

Use by audience, channel, and phase. Keep tone calm, precise, outcome-driven. No emojis, no buzzwords.

---

## Master (use everywhere)

| Tagline | When to use |
|--------|-------------|
| **Praxis turns intelligence into action, proof, and revenue.** | Primary brand line. Homepage, one-liners, open source README. |
| **Applied intelligence with accountability.** | Identity line. About, manifesto, positioning. |

---

## By narrative phase

### Phase 1 — Builders

| Tagline | Context |
|--------|---------|
| Run companies with agents, not just employees. | Hero, builder-focused landing. |
| Execution, verification, settlement—for agent labor. | Product/technical. |
| The management system for autonomous work. | Category definition. |
| Leverage. Ownership. Real outcomes. | Values / ethos. |

### Phase 2 — Markets

| Tagline | Context |
|--------|---------|
| Standards for trust, pricing, and settlement. | Market narrative. |
| Where agents are hired, routed, and paid. | Praxis Market. |

### Phase 3 — Society-scale

| Tagline | Context |
|--------|---------|
| Intelligence as an economic actor. Praxis as the infrastructure. | Governance / policy. |

## Anti-positioning (what we avoid)

- "AI-powered" / "powered by AI"  
- "Revolutionary" / "transformative"  
- Chatbot, assistant, or prompt-marketplace framing  
- Hype, futurist fluff, emojis

## One-liner cheat sheet

- **Shortest:** Applied intelligence with accountability.  
- **Product:** The management system for agent labor.  
- **Outcome:** Turns intelligence into action, proof, and revenue.  
- **Audience (builders):** Run companies with agents.  
- **Audience (enterprise):** Trust and settlement for autonomous work.
